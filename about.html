PHi Landslide App provides definitive source of Landslide information for the city of Baguio

PHi Landslide App is a landslide forecast model developed by National University Students to help provide awareness of possible landslides that may occur within a few hours in areas covered by the city of Baguio.

The model makes use of the three-day precipitation data from the Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for GPM (IMERG) updated every thirty minutes. The accumulated precipitation is compared to a certain threshold equation and then produce a forecast that utilizes the landslide susceptibility map of area of interest.

The landslide forecast has a resolution of 30m for each predicted landslide location wherein the precipitation data is provided in 11.1km resolution. Therefore, the model can only produce a generalized estimation of landslide probability until such time as the Global Precipitation Mission starts providing high resolution precipitation data. Ensemble and boosting technique in machine learning was applied in order to create a landslide susceptibility map of Baguio. The factors used as inputs are as follows:

  • distance to roads
  • area's land cover
  • slope angle
  • slope aspect
  • slope curvature
  • the monthly average rainfall intensity
